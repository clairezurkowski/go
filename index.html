Research interests:

Claire Zurkowski is a postdoctoral scholar in the Equilibrium Physics at Extreme Conditions (EPEC) group at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory. She received her Ph.D. in Geophysical Sciences from the University of Chicago in 2021 and was a postdoctoral fellow at the Earth and Planets Laboratory, Carnegie Instruction of Washington before joining EPEC in 2022. Claire specializes in the ultra-high-pressure crystallography, equations of state, and melting of metals and geomaterials using synchrotron multigrain and powder diffraction techniques in the laser-heated diamond anvil cell (DAC). She also works on designing toroidal diamond anvils to expand the accessible static pressure range to the multi-megabar regime. Claire’s work has been applied to a range of topics in physics and planetary sciences: including, the mineralogy and light element composition of Earth and planetary cores, chemical interactions at Earth’s core-mantle boundary, the thermodynamic history of meteorites and terrestrial diamond inclusions, and the ultra-high pressure equations of state of common pressure calibrants used in the DAC.
